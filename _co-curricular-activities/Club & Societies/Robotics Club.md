<h2>Robotics Club</h2>
<p>Welcome to the Robotics Club, where technology seamlessly integrates into
our daily lives, and the utilisation of Robotics and Artificial Intelligence
(AI) solutions is more vital than ever. In a world where automation and
robotic systems are revolutionising industries, our club offers an exciting
platform for students who are intrigued by the potential of robots and
their role in enhancing everyday life.</p>
<p>Using the LEGO MINDSTORM and SPIKE Prime sets alongside the approach grounded
in Design Thinking, a methodology that fosters creativity and innovation,
to guide our students to develop practical and meaningful solutions for
the challenges our society faces. We believe in harnessing the power of
robotics to address real-world problems, making daily life more efficient
and enjoyable for others.</p>
<p>Being a part of the Robotics Club means more than just tinkering with
robots; it means developing a profound understanding of emerging technologies,
honing your problem-solving skills, and experiencing hands-on learning
that prepares you for the challenges of the 21st century.</p>
<p>So, whether you're an aspiring engineer, a budding programmer, or simply
someone curious about the world of robotics, we invite you to be a part
of our vibrant community, where technology, creativity, and STEM education
converge to create a brighter future.</p>
<h4>Training Plan</h4>
<p><strong><u>Sec 1: Developing Foundations</u></strong>
</p>
<p>Sec 1 members will start by developing their foundational robotics skills
through instructor coaching, mentorship by seniors and participating in
Robotics competitions under the guidance of their seniors.</p>
<p><strong><u>Sec 2: Specialisation</u></strong>
</p>
<p>By Sec 2, members would have probably identified the area of specialisation
that they would like to focus on after getting a feel for robotics across
multiple fields. During this phase, members will participate in competitions
according to their area of specialisation and potential exco members will
be identified. Potential exco members will be tasked to shadow exco members
as they carry out their exco duties.</p>
<p><strong><u>Sec 3: Specialisation</u></strong>
</p>
<p>In Sec 3, members can either continue developing in their area of expertise
or seek to expand their skill sets by branching out into other areas. During
this phase, members will be tasked to lead a team on their own. New incoming
exco members will be appointed to replace out-going Sec 4 exco members
at the end of Term 2.</p>
<p><strong><u>Sec 4 and 5: Mentorship</u></strong>
</p>
<p>By Sec 4s/5s, they will be in charge of mentoring club juniors in preparation
for their competitions and exco duties. Sec 4 members will officially step
down from the CCA at the end of Term 2.</p>
